About Us

Founded in 1937 in Germany, Volkswagen—meaning "People's Car"—has grown into one of the most iconic and respected automotive brands in the world. What began as a vision to make car ownership accessible to everyday people has transformed into a global legacy of innovation, precision engineering, and reliability.

Volkswagen has continually shaped the automotive industry with legendary models like the Beetle, Golf, and Passat, while leading the way into the future with cutting-edge electric vehicles like the ID. series.

Volkswagen Legacy

The legacy of Volkswagen is built upon its reputation for engineering brilliance, affordable mobility, and cultural impact. Vehicles like the VW Beetle became symbols of freedom and individuality across the globe.

Today, the legacy continues with a strong focus on sustainability, through innovations like the ID.3 and ID.4 electric vehicles, aiming to redefine mobility for future generations.

Volkswagen Museum, Germany

Located in Wolfsburg, Germany, the Volkswagen AutoMuseum showcases the rich heritage of the brand. It houses over 130 classic vehicles, including prototypes and concept cars that never made it to production.

The museum offers a deep dive into Volkswagen's technological advancements and cultural milestones, making it a must-visit destination for auto enthusiasts and historians alike.
